Attic

A 3D project involving textures, lighting, modeling and set design.

category

Art

date

Fall 2013

technologies used

Autodesk 3ds Max

My attic project was my first exploration into the world of 3D. This is one of my first projects using a 3D modeling software, and I used this opportunity to learn how to model, texture, set dress and light a scene.

I had originally intended to make a whole animated short from this set. The idea was about antiquated, abandoned toys in the attic coming together to form a community, and venture out of the attic to other parts of the house below. It was to be set to old-timey music, most likely Les Feuilles Mortes by Edith Piaf. When paired with this music, I wanted the general ambience to be warm, dusty, yet solemn with tones of nostalgic sadness, of times forgotten. This is why I started modeling the house and a toy robot character, but ultimately this project was not completed.

Attic Inspiration

I really wanted the feeling of an attic that was crowded, dusty, and abandoned, yet wanted it to still have a warmth to it; an inviting quality to it that would make someone curious to explore it and discover the rich history hidden away in the depths of the disorganized piles laying around.

Creating the Attic

House Inspiration

I wanted the house to be cute, inviting and have a personality. One of my main inspirations was the charming house from Pixar's Up film.

Building the House

Robot Design (Unfinished Work in Progress)

Originally one of the main characters from my story was going to be an antique toy robot, inspired by the toys advertised in the 50s and 60s. With my limited 3D modeling experience, I tried my best to create a cute and simple, yet flexible and modular robot.
