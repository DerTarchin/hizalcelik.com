Floating Seat

Crafting a seat for a floating pod.

category

Art

date

Fall 2014

technologies used

Rhino3D, CNC Machine, Laser Cutter

course

3D Media Studio II: Robotic Tools

In this course we were tasked with using large scale crafting and materials tools like laser printers, CNC machines and other workshop tools. For my project, I wanted to make a floating "pod" that a user can sit and relax in while they float around. It proved to be a difficult task, as I didn't have enough time to test a smallscale size for any issues. It turns out, I forgot about simple physics of weight balancing on a floating device, which I get into in detail below.

Egg (Prototype)

Initially, I designed this pod to be egg-shaped and devised a structural shape that I assumed would be strong and symettrical. I designed it in CAD software and built it using a laser printer and cardboard.

However, I never had the chance to test it in water using waterproof materials. I also got to the next step, figuring out what material would "wrap around" the pod.

Ball

Due to time constraints, I cut back on my designs and borrowed a commercial inflatable ball, large enough for someone to stand in, and designed a seat to fit it. I designed the seat to have curves in all of the structurally strained areas to prevent the ball from ripping or bursting under pressure. I also designed it as multiple pieces that could be put together like a jigsaw puzzle.

This was never able to be tested in water, however, as I quickly realized that the seat was not symettrically balanced (even without a person sitting on it), and would tend to roll the ball backward.

The most difficult aspect of this project was actually getting all of the pieces into the ball through the slit designed for a person to enter with, and then inflating the ball with everything inside it.
