WatchBot

Experimental performances conducted with a remote control car.

category

Art

date

Spring 2016

technologies used

Modified Traxxas Slash 4x4

course

Intro to Contextual Practice

WatchBot was one of three public performance artworks, or "social experiments" (before it was a thing on YouTube), involving a remotely controlled scale vehicle (R/C car) and a variety of "influences," all performed on the campus of Carnegie Mellon University. From an unseen distance (3rd floor window of an art studio), I would casually follow or approach individuals and observe reactions to the different setups I created.

Surveillance - WatchBot

An R/C car designed to provoke emotional or physical responses with the concept of over-surveillance. One could either interpret this as surveillance and constant monitoring by government or private corporations, or perhaps an absurd take on overconsumption of media.

This was by far the most successful experiment of the three versions. The strongest response was an individual who walked circles around a tree, then sprinted back to they building they came from. You can view all reactions in the video below.

Talking

For this variation, I intended to instigate communication by attaching speakers to the vehicle and connecting them to an onboard phone, allowing me to communicate remotely with passerby. I spoke as if I were the car, saying things like "I'm down here!"

The noise of the car in motion made it difficult to hear the responses of passersby, but the attempt was generally successful and engaging. Many interested participants wanted to take selfies with the talking R/C car. You can view all reactions in the video below.

Music

In this experiment, I blasted either classical music or rap songs on small speakers. The intended effect was to represent people who drive around ballistic music from their cars, especially in crowded streets.

I wired a pair of speakers to an iPod and strapped them both to the car for this experiment. You can view all reactions in the video below.
