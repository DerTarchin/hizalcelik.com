Cake Lovers Anonymous

Re-editing the context of "That '70s Show".

category

Art, Video

date

Spring 2015

technologies used

Premiere Pro

course

Electronic Media: Moving Image

For this assignment, students were tasked with finding an existing scene from a show or film, and editing it in a way that changes the context of the scene entirely. My group decided to rewrite a scene from the show "That '70s Show," changing it to be a "Cake Lover's Anonymous" group meeting scene, where members try to convince each other to rise up over their cake eating addictions. You can watch the original scene here.

I ran into some difficulties editing this scene together. When filming, we had to make sure the background was a similar distance from the subjects, the lighting was dark and dusty, and the camera was positioned at a similar central point as the original scene.

Post-production proved to be even more difficult. Since we replaced the original character Fez with two of ours, the timing was off from the original, which was particularly affected by the audio track. I couldn't speed up the camera turn too much lest it stand out, and I couldn't cut the character shots too much otherwise the viewer barely has time to see our characters.

In the end, I was able to extend the scene every time we showed our characters by blending in a custom laugh track and royalty-free rock music with the original audio track. The result is a seamless extension of the scene.
