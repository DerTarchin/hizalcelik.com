Follower-Bot

Robotic car that chases specific colors.

category

Art, Code

date

Fall 2014

technologies used

Processing 2, modified Traxxas Slash 4x4, Pixy2 Camera, Arduino Uno

course

Electronic Media Studio: Interactivity

This project is a 1/10th scale RC car converted to follow specific colors and items. I purchased a large Superman cape with hopes of making this into a cool interactive project that can be put out in a public space and people can try running away from the robot. However due to technical difficulties and many issues, I’ve only been able to get my project working to a certain degree.

You can view the original project documentation on the class website.

The Technology

My project consists of three main parts; an Arduino to process the video footage and control the car, an RC car base and electronics (motor, servo, etc), and a Pixy2 camera (a specialized camera developed by CMULabs and built for Arduino, available here).

The Pixy2 camera follows the red of the Superman cape, and the Arduino measures the distance based on the pre-measured size of the cape. This is slightly problematic when the person wearing the cape runs faster and the cape flaps around, changing sizes.

The Wiring

Here is the Fritzing diagram for this project. Signals are being sent from the Arduino to the servo for steering and the ESC on my car for the motor speeds. The servo is powered by the ESC rather than the Arduino because the ESC outputs more voltage. The Arduino recieves power from a standard power source, but the ESC on the car is powered by a two cell LiPo battery. The two buttons control the base-size of the cape (resets distance) and the power (pressing the power starts the car, and press again to pause it, stopping all servo and motor use).
