Tell-e

A simple way to manage your movies and shows.

category

Code, UI/UX Design

date

Spring 2016

technologies used

Python, Django, HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, jQuery, Trakt.tv API, Heroku

Courses

Mobile IoT and Computing, Web Application Development

A webapp designed to help you track and manage your shows and movies. It had many features including alerts of upcoming release dates, managing custom lists of shows and movies, marking watched movies, episodes and shows, detailed info for media and actors, and more! A mobile and desktop version were made for different classes, and they do not share a database or code.

Mobile

The mobile version was created for a masters-level course focused on good mobile development practices. We conducted user surveys, case studies, and more while designing the app. We also created prototypes, wireframes and mock designs (including the motion graphic above). You can also view the live demo of the state of the product as of the end of the course, on the right.

Feel free to view the project report as well. It describes all the steps mentioned.

Desktop

For the browser-based implementation, this was for my Webapps class, the first time I'd ever made a webapp. My partner, Connor Maines, and I worked on building our first Django app, connecting the API of Trakt.tv, and mastering the concept of templating. What I learned in this project helped all future projects as well as inspire me to focus on front end development and webapp design.
