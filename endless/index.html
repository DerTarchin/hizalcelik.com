Experimental Science Fiction

This short film was entirely modelled, textured, lit and rendered within the 3D fractal-generation software called Mandelbulb 3D. There was no post-production aside from editing the clips together for the final film. I plan to make more scenes in the future and possibly create a set of full-size wallpapers along with another short film.

Endless Exploration

I learned quickly that a fractal-generated project would involve far less control than I'm used to, and it became more a matter of exploration. Instead of modelling an object like in traditional 3D software, these were already generated with mathematical equations, and one can zoom into them endlessly as they constantly regenerate.

I would "fly around" with the digital camera, spinning around and zooming in caves and corners until I lost all sense of orientation and depth.

From Equations to Artworks

Exploring interesting scenes and views was only part of the fun, the other was playing with lighting and colors. Simply by adjusting fog, atmosphere and texture color gradients (which the program gives little control over in terms of mapping locations), I could convert a complex spiral pattern into a wonderous forest, or an immersive cityscape, or a seemingly enourmous figure.
